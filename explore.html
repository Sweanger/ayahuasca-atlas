Ayahuasca Atlas

What the dataset looks like today — 1,232 organizations, read from their own words

All figures are self-described and unverified. Percentages of organizations are computed over the full set of 1,232; multi-select fields (org type, medicines) sum to more than 100%.

Where they operate

The field spans 46 countries, but concentrates in the Amazon source region and the Western markets that travel to it. Website language is the strongest single signal of who a site is written for — and it skews heavily English.

What they offer

A majority present as ceremonial or lineage-based rather than clinical or church — the category the original 4-type scheme couldn't see. Ayahuasca rarely travels alone: most centers work with several medicines. The median facilitating retreat runs about a week at ~$143 a night.

Who runs them

A striking split in gendered labor: named healers (the ceremonial / medicine role) skew male, while facilitators and support staff skew female. Gender is coded only from explicit signals — pronouns or gendered role terms — so the large “not determinable” share is the method refusing to guess from names.

How the dataset was built

Eight independent discovery channels feed the Atlas; because they recapture one another, a capture–recapture model estimates we currently see about half of the directory-visible facilitating population — the rest is structurally offline.
