Pipeline & Methodology

How the Ayahuasca Atlas is built, and how to trust it

The Atlas is a reproducible, multilingual dataset of organizations worldwide that publicly offer or facilitate ayahuasca. This document traces every stage a record passes through — from the search query that surfaced it to the frozen, versioned snapshot — and marks, at each step, what was captured, what a model inferred, and what a human verified.

Canonical scope statement

“The Ayahuasca Atlas is a directory, not a rating engine. All information is recorded as self-described and unverified unless explicitly marked as confirmed through the verification process.”

The lineage of a record

01The pipeline

Ten stages, each resumable and logged. Two of them are human.

Every datum carries its provenance. The colour of each stage below states how the values it produces are derived — the same legend used throughout the codebook:

Scraped LLM-coded Computed Human-validated Restricted in public deposit
1

Discovery Scraped

Five directory seeds (AyaAdvisors, Retreat.Guru, Third Wave, Safe Ceremonies, Psychedelic.Support), multilingual web search (EN·ES·PT), Google Places, a native-term sweep (yagé · daime · hoasca · caapi), and a snowball link-graph over captured sites. Every candidate is logged with its discovery_sources — so any single channel can be filtered out later, and cross-source overlap is measurable.

2

Capture Scraped

A polite crawler (honours robots.txt, per-domain rate limits, bounded backoff) fetches each page. Raw HTML is cached, the live page is submitted to the Wayback Machine at capture time (source_archive_url), and the search origin is recorded (scrape_origin) — provenance that cannot be reconstructed after a site changes or disappears.

3

Extraction LLM-coded

A language model reads the cached text under strict stated-only rules and emits one structured record. Claims stay claims; nulls are meaningful; nothing is inferred from imagery. Gender is coded only from explicit signals. See §4.

4

Normalization Computed

Prices converted to USD against a dated FX snapshot, per-night rates computed, locations geocoded. Arithmetic only — no new facts introduced.

5

Derivation Computed

The exclusive indigenous_affiliation_display bucket and the gender roll-ups are computed in code from the underlying claim fields by a documented first-match rule — never hand-entered or guessed by the model, so the projection is auditable and always reproduces from the same claims.

6

Deduplication Computed

The same organization found through several channels is merged on name similarity + geographic proximity + shared domain. Merged records union their offerings and their discovery_sources; nothing is silently dropped.

7

Classification LLM-coded

Organization type (wellness · religious · medical · ceremonial_traditional · other), individual-vs-organization, and facilitates_ayahuasca — the operational inclusion gate. This is the automated decision the relevance-gate study below validates.

8

Human validation Human-validated

Two independent coders, blind to the model, code stratified subsamples. Agreement (κ) is computed per field and per language before any reconciliation. Fields that fail their threshold are human-recoded before deposit.

Loop A — Relevance gate include / exclude

Coders judge whether each organization is in scope; compared against the model's facilitates_ayahuasca. Cohen's κ shows the inclusion rule is codable.

Loop B — Qualitative variables Tier-A fields

Coders code the interpretive fields (org type, the indigenous claim-cluster, sourcing, reciprocity, healer/facilitator split, presented gender) against a human gold standard. Per-field, per-language κ / α is the number reported in the dataset descriptor.

9

Coverage estimation Computed

Because discovery runs through independent channels, a capture–recapture model estimates the total population — including organizations never found — and therefore an honest coverage rate. See §6.

10

Freeze & deposit Restricted

A versioned snapshot is frozen once the coverage check passes, validated on the frozen object, then filtered through the sensitivity tiers (§7) to produce the public deposit — precise locations, revenue estimates, and named individuals held back for governed access.

What counts, and what doesn't

02The inclusion rule

One rule, two pathways, decided from an organization's own words.

An organization — center, church, collective, or individual practitioner — is included if, in its own public self-description, either of two pathways holds. Discovery deliberately over-collects; the rule is what narrows it, and Loop A measures whether two humans apply that rule the same way.

Pathway A — facilitation

It offers or facilitates ayahuasca in any form, including as a religious sacrament. facilitates_ayahuasca = true.

Pathway B — integration

It offers ayahuasca-specific integration or preparation services — the word “ayahuasca” appears in the service itself, even if no medicine changes hands.

  • Out of scope: generic wellness, therapy, or “psychedelic” work with no stated ayahuasca link; san-pedro / kambo / bufo-only operations; brokers and aggregators (used as discovery sources, never counted as organizations); one-off events.
  • Churches are in. Sacramental use is facilitation. A congregational church (Santo Daime, UDV) with no priced packages is recorded with an access model, not forced into a retreat shape — the absence of pricing is data.
  • Defunct sites are kept. Status is flagged, not deleted; longitudinal churn is signal.

The interpretive layer, disciplined

03Extraction under stated-only rules

The model records what a page says — not what it seems to imply.

Extraction is where a language model does the most work and therefore carries the heaviest validation burden. Three rules constrain it, and each is checked in the human study:

Claims stay claims

Indigenous involvement, community benefit, sourcing and reciprocity are captured with a _claimed suffix and, where possible, verbatim. A downstream user who never sees the site cannot mistake a marketing claim for a verified fact.

Gender from explicit signals only

A person's presented gender is coded only from an explicit statement, pronouns, or a grammatically gendered role term (curandera vs curandero) — never from a name, a photo, or appearance. No signal → not_determinable.

Nulls are meaningful

“Not stated” is kept distinct from “explicitly none.” A church with no listed price and a retreat that lists none are different facts, and the schema preserves the difference.

No affiliation verdict from the model

The model emits the underlying claims; the exclusive Indigenous-affiliation bucket is computed downstream by rule. The model never decides identity.

Observed in testing

Against live pages the rule holds: a page naming “Maestra Monika” is coded female (gendered term); “Steve,” male (pronoun); but “Teri,” “Tata,” and “Capitán” are left not_determinable — the model does not guess gender from a name.

Treating the model as a coder

04Human-vs-LLM validation

Automated coding is scalable; validating it is how it earns trust.

The resolution is standard and publishable: treat the model as one coder and validate it against a human gold standard. Two people independently code stratified subsamples, blind to the model and to each other. We report inter-human agreement (the task is well-defined) and model-vs-human agreement (the model is accurate) — computed before reconciliation, so the numbers describe exactly the deposited data.

The threshold is explicit: high agreement (κ ≳ 0.75) validates the model for that field at scale; low agreement sends that field — and only that field — to human coding. Coders work from the same cleaned page text the model saw, delivered as blind spreadsheets with the model's answers held in a separate key. Every value is traceable to whether a human or the model produced it.

How much of the field do we see?

05Coverage, estimated honestly

The organizations we can't find are part of the finding.

Independent discovery channels recapture one another, which lets a capture–recapture model (log-linear multiple-systems estimation) estimate the total population — including organizations no channel found. The directory-visible facilitating population is estimated at roughly half-covered and stable: each new discovery lever surfaces fewer net-new centers while the estimated population holds, which is itself the result — the web-visible field is near-saturated, and its remainder is structurally offline.

Four filters between the field and the dataset

06The bias framework

Each level is a distinct agent of distortion — kept separate, not blurred into “data is biased.”

Level 1

The medium's filter

What a website omits or spins. Answered by the _claimed architecture and unverifiable sourcing/reciprocity fields.

Level 2

Broadcasting as selection

Having a web presence at all is non-random — it over-represents English-language, Western-facing operations. Measured across language, org type, and region; the same gradient reappears in the economic data.

Level 3

The observer's imposition

Aggregating and publishing creates a legibility that did not exist, done by non-Indigenous researchers. Answered by the positionality statement, the canonical scope sentence, and the capture-only architecture.

Level 4

The review skew

Who writes online reviews skews Western / tourist / extreme-valence — compounding Level 2 rather than correcting it. Handled as a separate, IRB-gated data regime.

Open where it characterizes, closed where it targets

07What is public, and what is held back

The project's answer to the tension between open data and Indigenous data governance.

The deposit distinguishes three tiers: the live site, the frozen public deposit, and an internal analytic tier that is collected but not published. The principle is to deposit what characterizes the field and withhold what would target the individuals or organizations within it — CARE-aligned caution appropriate to an unaffiliated, pre-partnership project.

CARE obligations attach with particular force to the Indigenous-data subset — the fields recording Indigenous identity, knowledge, or representation. For those, the project practices what an unaffiliated team can unilaterally: verbatim capture with no imposed taxonomy, restricted deposit of named individuals, a correction-and-dispute channel, and a stated collective-benefit intention — and names plainly what remains owed: community governance and authority to control, which require a partnership not yet in place.
